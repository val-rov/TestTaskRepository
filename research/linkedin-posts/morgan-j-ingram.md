## Chasing AI Efficiency Is About to Set B2B Sales Back 10 Years

**Date:** Wed, 10 Jun 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/morganjingramamp_my-take-is-that-the-way-werechasing-ai-activity-7470448451105759232-L1x9?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

My take is that the way we're chasing AI efficiency is about to set the B2B sales profession back 10 years.

Before the pitchforks come out.... hear me out. I'm deep in AI... I love me some Claude and I use it every day.

I understand the goal of AI is to make your 20-person team operate like 40. That's the multiplier and I'm all for it.

And... I know your board is asking about AI, which creates executive urgency to adopt AI at a faster rate.

Where my take is coming from though is I went to a conference last week and every conversation was the same... "AI is going to free up our reps' time."

Ok yes we agree....cool... I kept asking this follow up question.

Free up time to do what exactly?

"More selling." "More calling."

Ok but that's like one hour.. then what? What's the actual game plan when reps have more free time?

Crickets.

Because we all know the way things are moving the AI transformation will happen fast.. Two quarters from now the AI layer will be table stakes. Every team will have it and every competitor will be running the same playbooks.

Which means the only thing that separates your team from theirs is how your people show up.

The last thing we want on our sales team is them losing critical thinking skills and never learning business acumen which would upset a future buyer.

As sales leaders we need to imagine beyond just selling:
→ Tailored campaigns with direct mails and multi-threading
→ Spending time learning the industry with podcasts and increasing business acumen
→ Live engagement in communities where your buyers actually spend time
→ Getting in the field... events, dinners, faces across tables

That's the game after the AI dust settles guiding the reps to be more magnetic, intuitive and consistent humans and I'm already obsessing over it.

For example, I've been locked in on dropping body fat for the last 8 months. That commitment is specific. So the activity is specific... walking 10k steps daily, pickleball, lots of protein, working out 3x a week.

The commitment defined the activity.

Your AI strategy needs the same thing. Don't ONLY focus on freeing up time. Define what the freed time is FOR before you deploy the tools.

Bookmark this... Come back in 6-8 months... I promise you the conversation on every sales floor will be: "How do I get my reps to think again and not talk like a robot?"

The leaders who built the human muscle DURING the AI transformation won't have that problem.

The question was never "how do we free up time."

The question is "what are we freeing it for?"

If we can all answer that, we'll have a better sales community.

P.S. Sales leaders... what's your team actually doing with the time AI is freeing up? I've been asking this at every event and nobody has a clear answer yet. Prove me wrong in the comments.

## This One Claude Prompt Replaced 3 Hours of Buyer Research

**Date:** Tue, 09 Jun 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/morganjingramamp_this-one-claude-prompt-replaced-3-hours-ofactivity-7470114548918841344-Rm6?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

This one Claude prompt replaced 3 hours of buyer research for every team I work with down to 5 minutes.

I timed it.. so no lies here.

So most of the teams that you coach and lead find a signal that says... "I see your company just raised funding"... type stuff that every other rep is also saying.

So I built a prompt that does the deep research in about 5 minutes. And I mean actually deep... not some fluff insight.

I'm talking specific challenges tied to the role.... What buying signals happen within your inbound process.. Trigger events that signal buying readiness... Even the reasons they might resist your solution so your reps aren't blindsided on calls.

Here's the prompt:

"You are a B2B intelligence expert with 20+ years of experience researching buyer personas and creating targeted messaging strategies. Conduct a deep dive on the following persona: [insert Persona] in [insert Industry].

Identify the top 3 pain points most relevant to this persona's role and business context in (current year). Look at my inbound conversations and find 3 correlation points. List 3 trigger events that typically create readiness to buy. Outline 3 reasons this persona might resist your solution.

Ensure all output is practical, specific, and grounded in real-world B2B dynamics. Now explain this to me so I know you understand the process."

So.. I run this inside Claude as a project. That way the context stays loaded and every rep on the team can access it without re-entering the setup every time.

But here's the part most people miss and it drives me crazy...

Don't have your teams just copy and paste this....

Because AI doesn't know your buyer.... AI knows patterns about your buyer. ...The rep's job is to take that intelligence and make it HUMAN... to connect a dot the AI couldn't see.

That's the workflow we build inside our program Sales Team Six.

The teams using this workflow are starting more conversations because their messages sound like someone who actually studied the buyer.

Not someone who prompted a bot and hit send.
