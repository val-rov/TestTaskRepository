## Launching an AI SDR Chatbot: Early Results

**Date:** Thu, 01 Aug 2024
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/activity-7224809529358626818-tjUi?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

We recently added on Qualified's AI SDR chatbot functionality, going live in the first stage of our stomach scoot, crawl, walk, run, sprint plan.

(Shoutout to Danielle Lipschutz, our CSM, for coining the stomach scoot)

After training our AI SDR on over 80+ common questions and giving her access to all of our webpages + content, it's really cool to see how she navigates conversations with live prospects.

After just a week on the earliest stage of our plan, we've seen:
- 55% conversion rate from conversation to booked meeting
- 86% conversion rate from meeting offered to booked meeting
- A healthy amount of deflection for non-converting site visitors (i.e. those who are looking for career opps or info that doesn't require a meeting)

Personally, this is my first experience implementing a bigger AI tool like this, and it's been exciting seeing how the conversations are being handled when questions come up.

Figured I'd share this conversation that happened in the wee hours of this morning with a potential prospect, because it's too good not to share.

Apparently, she's equipped to take big, philosophical questions and tie it back to our company's value props.

## On Tone-Deaf Subject Lines

**Date:** Thu, 15 Jun 2023
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/activity-7075176355155333120-lS4x?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

I received an email today with this subject line - oof

don't get me wrong - subject lines are important, because you want to get folks to open

but - at what cost are you willing to use a controversial subject line to get that open?

personally, I'll never encourage my team to use a subject line that comes across as tone deaf or insensitive to current market conditions and personal experiences

even if I think it'll increase our open rates exponentially

so, just don't do it

(fwiw - the content inside had nothing to do with layoffs)
