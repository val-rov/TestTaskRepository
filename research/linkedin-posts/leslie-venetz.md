## Stop Paying for Outdated Sales Advice

**Date:** Sat, 13 Jun 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_earntheright-activity7471577133425967104-JVrz?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

Stop paying for outdated sales advice.

I consume a LOT of sales training content.

I love to learn and the more I learn the more I realize there is to learn ... IYKYK.

Over and over again many the same thing keeps showing up.

Many of the frameworks being used or the techniques between taught are old and outdated.

They are built on sales training manuals that IBM, Xerox, and AT&T put out in the 1970s..

They pull from books like Tom Hopkins' How to Master the Art of Selling, wildly popular in the 1980s and 90s.

Here's what's confusing -

Just because something is old does NOT mean that it is outdated.

Which leaves sellers, especially new sellers, asking - how do I know if this sales advice that people having been giving/taking for 50+ years still applies to the modern buyer?

What makes it harder is that these folks are usually pretending like they have a silver bullet - some magic way to get you 30 meetings a month or to 10x your revenue or ramp every single rep in 14 days.

The alluring promises combined with advice that sounds familiar is a dangerous combo.

Here's what I see (re)surfacing way too often (especially on Reddit/TikTok/IG content and courses)-

For DECADES, salespeople were taught to aggressively prospect, bulldoze over objections, and push for a close at all costs.

That approach was designed for a buyer who had no choice but to talk to a rep to get information.

That buyer does not exist anymore.

Today's buyer has done the research before your rep gets the meeting.

They are not waiting to be persuaded.

They are deciding if a sales rep can #EarnTheRight to their time an attention by showing up with insights and to act as a trusted advisor.

Here's the test to know if the external expertise you're paying for is built with the modern buyer / 2026 trust economy in mind -

- Does the advice assume the seller has more information than the buyer and use that assumption to gatekeep info from the buyer?
- Does it treat the buyer as someone to be manipulated?
- Does it reward the rep for activity rather than strategy that converts?

That is far from an exhaustive list, but should get your wheels turning.

What is one thing from your early sales training you later had to completely unlearn?

For me it was to not bulldoze objections.

## Saying Something True Doesn't = Personalization

**Date:** Wed, 10 Jun 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_saying-something-true-doesntpersonalization-activity-7470573007787319296-LYxq?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

Saying something true doesn't = personalization.

"Congrats on your Series A!" is true, but that doesn't make it personalization.

I audit a LOT of sales messaging and 90% of the time the email that follows that message could literally have been sent to any company at any stage in any industry.

The funding round was just a disguise for a generic email. That's still SPAM.

"I noticed you're the VP of Sales at XYZ" is true, but is it relevant?

They know their own title.

Showing that you how to read a person's job title from their LinkedIn profile doesn't earn the right to their attention.

That is not personalization.

Real personalization at scale is about finding something relevant to say.

They know what their job title is. What they don't know is why that's relevant to the outcomes you provide.

They know they just raised a round. What they don't know how the dots connect between that raise and the benefits of your product.

A true but irrelevant detail stapled to a generic pitch is still a generic pitch.

## Most Sales Sequences Are Activity Trackers Disguised as a Cold Outreach Strategy

**Date:** Tue, 09 Jun 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_most-sales-sequences-are-activitytrackers-activity-7470225727800516608-3Md8?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

Most sales sequences are activity trackers disguised as a cold outreach strategy

What I start working with sales teams, one of the first things I like to audit are their sequences.

I want to figure out what is working so we can do more of it and what's not earning the attention of the buyer so we can iterate.

When I review sequences that *should* work because they follow the rules of 8 + steps and they are multi-channel and the emails are scoring 96% etc but aren't consistently booking quality meetings, this is what I find -

1. The sequences asked for too much too early.

They were built to demand conversion before earning the right to it.

2. They don't feel cohesive.

A great sequences tells a cohesive story. Low-converting sequences look like a bunch of random steps and copy smashed together.

Here are 2 quick fixes that you can test if either of those problems sounds familiar:

Escalating CTAs & deposits

Start with low friction asks like "reply yes if you'd like XYZ," or "Any interest" and as you build credibility and relevance throughout the sequence escalate to more direct asks for attention and time.

+ use deposits which is the idea of giving some valuable before you ask your prospect for anything

Break down your bullet points

If you're using a cold email with bullet points, you're trying to say too much in a single email.

The great news is that single email can become your full sequence. Make each bullet point into it's own email with a focus on the BENEFITS to the prospect.

Remember seller-centric and feature-forward emails don't convert. Write about the outcomes related to each bullet point and then thread those 3 emails to deepen the insights you share.

EG email 1 = bullet point 1, email 2 = bullet point 1 threaded etc

What your favorite sequence quick fix?

BONUS content if you scrolled this far -

Stop using "thoughts?" emails.

They are lazy AF and only get replies because the data shows that 90% + of outreach ends way to early.

Replace thoughts with, "Leslie - What are your thoughts about how many TikTok shop videos are on the FYP?"

## Spray and Pray Has Never Worked Less Than It Does Right Now

**Date:** Mon, 08 Jun 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_spray-and-pray-has-never-worked-lessthan-activity-7469765213974749185-YM4M?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

Spray and pray has never worked less than it does right now.

When I starting working with sales teams, one of the most common things I see is a team that is genuinely working hard, but not getting results.

They are smashing the phone, sending 100+ weekly connections on LinkedIn, sending out thousands of emails every week and have almost nothing to show for it.

The issue is almost always the same.

The team is using identical or almost identical message for all of their accounts.

Y'ALL I get the unexplainable urge that makes it feel like you should have somehow contacted every single lead in your territory and it should have ben done yesterday.

Believe me - I do, but it does not work. Ever & it has a higher cost than just lack of immediate results.

Attempting to contact as many leads as possible as quickly as possible has an internal cost and an external cost.

- Internally, reps get overwhelmed chasing hundreds of accounts with no clear strategy.

Conversion rates drop, confidence takes a hit, and frustration rises.

Instead of investing energy in deliberate skill development, they get distracted by doing more.

They start putting up activity numbers instead of generating meaningful conversations, their quota suffers and so does retention.

- Externally, blasting your entire TAM with irrelevant messages damages your brand reputation with the buyers you actually want to reach.

Buyers do not see value in generic outreach.

They feel harassed by it & will mark messages as SPAM / block email addresses faster than ever before.

AI has made it easier than ever to send more messages faster, which means the bar for relevance has never been higher if you want to earn and maintain trust with your prospects.

MORE IS NOT A STRATEGY

High activity and weak pipeline is a direction problem, not an effort problem.

If your team is struggling to get results before I ask for more effort or activity I'd would -

1. Evaluate my territory to make sure it only has good fit accounts
2. Do a segmentation exercise so reps know how to break down their territory into manageable lists
3. Teach them how to build segment specific value props so their messaging is relevant

What's the first thing you look at when a rep isn't getting good enough results form their hard work?

## Your Team Has 3x Pipeline Coverage and Is Still Missing Quota

**Date:** Wed, 03 Jun 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/leslievenetz_your-team-has-3x-pipeline-coverage-and-isactivity-7468028741416288256-Inzg?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

Your team has 3x pipeline coverage and is still missing quota.

I bet 1 or all 3 of these culprits is at play.

1. You do not have clear qualification criteria.

The result: Your reps are opening opportunities with bad-fit accounts or prospects who are never going to close.

2. Auto-opening opps

You've fallen into the trap of auto-opening opportunities in your CRM the moment a discovery meeting is marked complete.

Y'all I LOATHE this practice because the opp gets opened whether or not there is also intent or even ability to purchase in the near term.

The result: Your pipeline is bloated with fake opps.

3. No next steps

If your reps are not setting mutually agreed-to and clear next steps at the end of a discovery call - what are we even doing here?

The result: There might be 3x coverage, but very few opportunities with specific next steps that will drive a deal forward.

The fix is not more pipeline. It is better gates at the top of it.

What's your goal for coverage now? 3-5x still?
