## Why You Need to Start Methodically Forecasting Pipeline After $5M ARR

**Date:** Thu, 11 Jun 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/florintatulea_s-activity-7470910227299778560-0JjE?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

This is why you need to start methodically forecasting pipeline after $5M ARR (and how to do it):

Forecasting is something that all sales leaders do with their AEs. But it's not talked enough about at the pipegen level.

Forecasting is important because it let's you understand where you are likely to land in the future and let's you take action and have line of sight into what can change before it happens.

All of this information already exists in your CRM.

If you don't already do this you 100% should. Especially after $5m ARR…

By this point you should have a Rev Ops person that can help you pull this, but if not, you CAN do it yourself.

Here are the key things you need to track to run proper forecasting.

1. Across all your different pipe gen streams you need to track:

-Stage 1 (meeting booked) to S2 (Qualified opp) conversion %
-ASP (Average Sales Price)
-Time from S1 to S2
-Meeting to S2 in weeks

2. This allows you to forecast what your pipeline and S2 #s are going to look like for the following week.

The numbers in this table allow you to have line of sight into how many S2s you are forecasting each week, and what the pipeline $ looks like.

It's important that you do this per cohort and are actually tracking the funnel for each batch of opportunities.

I usually do forecasting based on First Meeting Date.

IT'S ALSO EXTREMELY IMPORTANT THAT YOU MEASURE YOUR FORECAST DELTA.

This allows you to see how well you have a grasp on your business.

More importantly, forecasting like this helps you take action.. If you are projecting a bad forecast for the next several weeks, it allows you to take action TODAY to impact the number moving forward.

Now you actually have a plan and you look much better in front of your exec team.

## If You Can't Do Discovery in an Interview, Why Would I Trust You With Our Prospects?

**Date:** Tue, 26 May 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/florintatulea_if-you-cant-do-discovery-in-an-interview-activity7465061518737465345-c_Mh?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

If you can't do discovery in an interview, why would I trust you to do it with our prospects?

Asking 2-3 generic questions as a salesperson in an interview is DIABOLICAL.

The part of the interview where you ask questions is about HALF of what you are being judged on.

First of all, your job in sales is to do discovery, be curious and uncover information/pain. If you aren't doing it in an interview, you likely aren't doing it in your job.

Second of all, salary and OTE are NOT the only thing that matters.

You need to interview the company, opportunity and the hiring managers as well.

Broken down into 3 sections, here are a few great questions:

1. SDR related
2. Company related
3. Manager related

SDR Related (can ask similar for AE roles):

-What is the average tenure on your SDR team? Are you happy with that as a manager?
-What % of your SDRs are hitting quota?
-How many SDRs got promoted to AEs in the last year?
-What is your average ramp time?
-What does your top SDR do that your other SDRs don't?

Company related:

-Who is your top competitor and what's the main reason you are losing to them?
-Is marketing incentivized on MQLs or on closed revenue?
-What is your Net Revenue Retention? (110%+ means the business is generally in a good place)
-YoY revenue growth in the past 2 years? Projections for this year?
-What is the biggest threat to your business in the next 2 years?

Manager Related:

-What is your management philosophy?
-How often do you hold 1:1s with your team?
-What does the onboarding program look like?
-What kind of training do you have in place for SDRs?
-Do I have the ability to craft my own messaging and adapt as I see fit?

If you ask these questions you automatically will stand out above 99% of sales reps.

You don't have to ask them all on one call btw.

Do it throughout the interview process.

## Your Intent Data Has a Dirty Secret

**Date:** Tue, 12 May 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/florintatulea_sales-outbound-ai-activity-7460004386682019840-gA_X?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

Your intent data has a dirty secret.

It tells you what company is potentially in-market.

However, it has no idea who is causing the intent.

So your reps get a list of accounts, fire off a sequence, and hope someone bites.

That's not a signal-based motion. That's spray and pray with extra steps.

Here's the workflow I'd build instead:

Start with G2 intent.

When an account spikes on G2, category browsing, competitor comparisons, review reading — that's a real behavioral signal.

They're likely actively evaluating your space.

But G2 intent is account-level. You know the company is in-market. You still don't know who's holding the shortlist.

Now layer in contact-level website ID (Available in Zoominfo).

Cross-reference that against your G2 surge accounts.

If someone from that account is hitting your pricing page, your integrations docs, or your "vs. competitor" page... you just went from a company signal to a person signal.

G2 intent tells you the account might be in an active buying cycle. Contact-level ID tells you who inside that account is doing the research.

One without the other is a guess.

Together, they're a prioritization engine.

Here's the mental model I use:

→ G2 surge = the fire alarm went off at that account
→ Website visitor ID = here's the person who pulled the alarm

You're not cold calling anymore.

You're calling the exact person who walked through your front door, compared you to three competitors, and read your pricing page twice.

That's not outbound. That's a warm conversation with context.

## Everybody Is Using the Same Playbook. That's the Problem.

**Date:** Fri, 08 May 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/florintatulea_outbound-sales-activity-7458512843706019840-fpus?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFWF4egBCw1RxVdlCsF4TTgWYOsP4Y-eeyE

Everybody is using the same playbook. That's the problem.

Do this instead…

Sit down with your team and reverse engineer all of your closed won deals in the last year..

Go back in time:

-3-6 months for SMB/Mid-Market
-12-18 months for Enterprise

What were the signs, signals, catalysts that got your customers to actually buy?

You can even get Claude to analyze all of your transcripts to find patterns here.

Pipegen is a game of timing.

Signals are meant to help you predict who to reach out to at the right time with the right message.

See.. Most GTM leaders are asking the completely wrong question.

It doesn't matter what signals/plays the best performing teams are using.

The absolute BEST signals and plays are unique to your company and market.

Most signals that you see on here are completely commoditized at this point.

Especially when used in isolation…

-Funding
-Headcount growth
-Job Changes
-Generic Intent

The best orgs that I have seen are looking for very specific information that informs their outreach.

1. If you are a Waste Management Company

Looking for orgs that produce X tons of waste in a given year (watch my video from Monday to see how you can do this at scale)

2. Company that detects Software Outages?

Looking for orgs that have had software outages in a given location within the last 3 months

3. Company that sells Restaurant Software?

Looking for restaurants with low Google ratings and using AI agents to pick out themes of negative reviews

4. Medical Waste and Compliance Training?

Locating high-growth veterinary clinics that have low-compliance ratings and high growth

Most companies are sitting on GOLD within their first party data (CRM, Call Transcripts, Product Usage data)

Take some time to understand WHY your customers bought and go and build these systems.

It's how you truly stand out.
